Text Analytics: Climate and Twitter NER

End-to-end NLP coursework covering climate sentiment classification, topic modelling of risk and opportunity disclosures, and named entity recognition on Twitter. The project compares classic baselines with transformer methods and reports entity-level metrics suitable for real extraction tasks.

View repo View report (PDF)

Problem

Extract actionable information from climate disclosures and noisy social text. Build quick yet reliable classifiers for sentiment, surface themes with topic modelling, and evaluate named entity recognition with strict span-level metrics.

Approach

  • Climate sentiment: compared Naive Bayes, a small neural net, and a compact transformer on ClimateBERT samples, tuning n-grams and minimum document frequency for best balance.
  • Topic modelling: used LDA on risk and opportunity subsets with Count and TF-IDF vectorisation, measuring coherence and interpretability.
  • Twitter NER: fine-tuned bert-base-cased for BIO tagging on the BTC dataset, reporting precision, recall, and F1 at the entity level.

Results

  • Climate sentiment: Naive Bayes reached 79.0% accuracy, outperforming the neural and transformer baselines on this low-resource setup.
  • LDA topics: TF-IDF input achieved higher coherence (0.4445) than Count (0.4347), producing sharper, domain-specific topics.
  • Twitter NER: Overall entity-level F1 77.9%, with PER 88.7%, LOC 67.1%, ORG 57.4%; token-level F1 77.03%.

Tech stack

Python, pandas, scikit-learn, matplotlib, transformers (Hugging Face). LDA topic modelling and reproducible Jupyter notebooks with fixed seeds.
