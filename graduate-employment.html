Graduate Employment Visual Analytics

Regional analysis of graduate employment across England and Wales using harmonised census data for 2011 and 2021. Tableau storytelling highlights spatial patterns and change over time. Python analytics uses PCA and Bayesian Ridge to forecast 2030 outcomes.

View repo View report (PDF)

Problem

Graduate outcomes vary widely by region. Policymakers and universities need a clear view of where employment is improving, where it is lagging, and how the structure of local economies relates to future prospects.

Approach

  • Harmonised 2011 and 2021 census variables to 292 local authority districts on 2021 boundaries.
  • Tableau stories with choropleths, ranked bars, and diverging bars to communicate level and change.
  • PCA on normalised indicators to reveal structural similarity. Bayesian Ridge used to forecast graduate employment to 2030 with uncertainty bands.

Results

  • Clear urban advantage and persistent coastal and rural pockets of lower graduate employment growth.
  • PCA separated regions into coherent clusters that align with industry mix and higher education density.
  • Bayesian Ridge produced stable 2030 forecasts and credible intervals suitable for planning and scenario discussion.

Tech stack

Tableau, Python, pandas, scikit-learn (PCA, BayesianRidge), matplotlib. Clean data joins and reproducible preprocessing in notebooks.
